# H13 — The closed-loop related-party web

**Thesis under test:** Musk's entities form a closed loop in which value, services, assets, and credit circulate *between companies he controls*, with the same individuals sitting on multiple sides of each transaction. The web is disclosed but not legible, and it routes value toward the operator and his private vehicles.

**Status: SUPPORTED (directly, from Tesla's own proxy).** Provenance: `b` = primary filing; `a` = our reading.

Source: Tesla 2025 DEF 14A, "Certain Relationships and Related Person Transactions" (filed 2025-09-17), `sources/tesla/def14a_2025.txt`, lines 8416–8433. SpaceX side cross-referenced from the SPCX dossier (S3).

---

## 1. Tesla's disclosed related-party flows (FY2024 + early 2025)

Every row below is a transaction *between Tesla and another entity Elon Musk controls or is affiliated with* — disclosed in Tesla's own proxy. `b`.

| Counterparty (Musk-controlled / affiliated) | Flow direction | Amount (2024) | Nature |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SpaceX** | Tesla → SpaceX | ~$2.4M | commercial, licensing, support agreements |
| **SpaceX (aircraft)** | Tesla → SpaceX | ~$0.8M | Tesla rents a SpaceX-owned aircraft (since Apr 2016) |
| **X (formerly Twitter)** | Tesla → X | ~$0.1M + ~$0.4M ads | commercial/consulting + Tesla buying advertising on X |
| **The Boring Company (TBC)** | Tesla → TBC | ~$3.6M | commercial agreements |
| **Musk-owned security company** | Tesla → Musk entity | ~$2.8M | security services for Musk (partial cost) |
| **Redwood Materials** (CEO: JB Straubel, Tesla director) | Tesla → Redwood (scrap) | ~$30.3M | Tesla supplies scrap; Redwood incurred the expense |
| **Nova Sky Stories** (CEO: Kimbal Musk) | Tesla → Nova | ~$0.3M | aerial show production |
| **xAI** | **xAI → Tesla** | **~$198.3M** (of which **~$191.0M** is **Megapack** purchases) | xAI buys Tesla Megapacks + consulting/support |

### The standout: Tesla → xAI Megapacks (~$191M)
Tesla (public; Musk ~20%) **sold ~$191M of Megapack products in 2024 to xAI** (private; Musk-controlled). This is the single largest related-party line, and it runs *from* the public company *to* the private one as a customer relationship — i.e., a Musk private entity is a material customer of the public company he runs. Whether favorable or not, price discovery here is internal to the loop. `b`

### The governance overlay
- **JB Straubel** (Tesla director) is CEO of **Redwood**, Tesla's scrap counterparty (~$30.3M). Director on one side, counterparty-CEO on the other. `b`
- Proxy Proposal Seven (2025): a shareholder proposal asking the board to authorize a **Tesla investment in xAI** — the board acknowledges this "would likely be considered a related party transaction." Tesla considering buying into Musk's *other* AI company is the loop trying to close further. `b`
- Nov-2021 **indemnification agreement**: Musk indemnifies Tesla for additional taxes owed on the 2012 CEO award exercise — an insider/issuer financial entanglement. `b`

## 2. The same shape at SpaceX (cross-ref SPCX dossier S3 — "Valor")

The Tesla web is the *public, mature* version of what the SPCX dossier found at SpaceX, where one director (Antonio Gracias / Valor) is simultaneously:
- **2nd-largest shareholder** (~500M shares),
- **lessor** on ~$20B of equipment leases to SpaceX's AI subs (~$9B on balance sheet as SpaceX-guaranteed debt), and
- a member of the **board and compensation committee**.

Gracias also appears in the **SolarCity** story (recused alongside Musk on the 2016 Tesla vote — see `H11`). The *same names* recur across Tesla, SolarCity, and SpaceX as owner, creditor, and pay-setter.

## 4. Falsifiers (what would break H13)

- If Tesla's related-party section were empty or de-minimis → H13 weakened. **Not the case** (~$235M+ of disclosed inter-entity flows in 2024 alone).
- If the same individuals did *not* recur across owner/creditor/board roles → H13 weakened. **Not the case** (Musk, Gracias, Straubel, the Rives all multi-hatted).
- If transactions were independently priced via arm's-length bids → partial rebuttal. Disclosure says terms are "no less favorable than" third-party, but pricing is internal; not independently verifiable from the filing. `a`

**One line:** Tesla's own proxy documents a live, ~$235M/yr web of transactions among Musk-controlled entities, with directors sitting on multiple sides — the disclosed, public-company proof of the same structure the SPCX dossier found at SpaceX, and the same names that recused on SolarCity.
