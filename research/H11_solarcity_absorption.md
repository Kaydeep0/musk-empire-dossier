# H11 — Assemble-by-absorption: the SolarCity → Tesla precedent

**Thesis under test:** When a Musk entity is in distress, it is *absorbed* into a larger Musk-controlled vehicle via a related-party, common-control-flavored combination — and its history/debt is restated into the acquirer rather than priced and resolved in the open market. The SpaceX-by-absorption story (Twitter→X→xAI→SpaceX) has a clean, fully-documented dress rehearsal: **SolarCity → Tesla, 2016.**

**Status: SUPPORTED — strongest historical test.** Provenance: `b` = primary filing; `c` = public record.

Sources: SolarCity FY2015 10-K (`sources/solarcity/10k_fy2015.txt`); Tesla/SolarCity joint merger proxy DEFM14A, 2016-10-12 (`sources/solarcity/merger_proxy_2016.txt`).

---

## 1. The distress (what was being absorbed)

SolarCity, FY2015 (10-K, `b`):
- **Net loss: $768.8M** for 2015.
- **Operating cash burn: $789.9M** used in operations during 2015.
- **Cash on hand: $382.5M** (Dec 31, 2015) — *under six months* of runway at the 2015 burn rate.
- **Total debt: $2,753,746K ≈ $2.75B** (recourse + non-recourse), heavily securitized through SPEs.
- Rapidly rising cash burn: operating cash used went $174.5M *generated* (2013) → $217.8M *used* (2014) → $789.9M *used* (2015). The trajectory was accelerating, not stabilizing.

This is a company that needed capital urgently and on terms a distressed public market would have punished.

## 2. The related-party structure (who sat on which side)

From the merger proxy (`b`):
- **Elon Musk**: Chairman & largest shareholder of **SolarCity** *and* CEO/largest shareholder of **Tesla** — both sides of the deal.
- **The Rive brothers** (Lyndon Rive, CEO; Peter Rive) — **Musk's cousins** — co-founded and ran SolarCity. (Their 2015 founder options were cancelled for no consideration in the merger.)
- **Antonio Gracias**: director with ties to both — recused alongside Musk.
- **Recusal as tell:** "the Tesla Board, with Messrs. Elon Musk and Antonio Gracias recusing themselves, approved the Merger Agreement." A recusal is an admission that the conflict was real enough to require stepping out of the vote. `b`
- **Insider debt holdings:** as of Sept 23, 2016, Tesla directors/officers as a group held **25,004,557 SolarCity shares**, **$10M of zero-coupon convertible senior notes (due 2020)**, and **$65M of Solar Bonds (due Feb 17, 2018)**. Insiders were SolarCity *creditors* being made whole by the public company's acquisition. `b`
- **SpaceX** separately purchased SolarCity Solar Bonds (~$165M+ across tranches, widely reported) — a third Musk entity providing related-party financing that kept SolarCity liquid into the merger. `c`

## 3. The "restate," not "price" mechanism

- The deal was **all-stock** (~$2.6B; exchange ratio of Tesla shares for SolarCity shares), so no independent cash price was tested in the market. `c`
- Two fairness opinions were procured — **Evercore** (Tesla) and **Lazard** (SolarCity special committee) — the governance scaffolding used to make a related-party combination defensible. `b`
- **$2.75B of SolarCity debt was recast onto Tesla's balance sheet** and SolarCity's operations folded into "Tesla Energy." The distress did not get resolved by markets; it got *absorbed and restated* into a larger, more credible issuer Musk also controlled. `b`/`a`
- **Litigation:** shareholders sued, alleging the Tesla board breached fiduciary duty by bailing out a distressed, Musk-affiliated company ("the Actions"). Musk ultimately prevailed at trial (Delaware, 2022), but the suit's existence confirms the market read this as a related-party rescue. `c`

## 4. Why this is the template for SpaceX-by-absorption

| Feature | SolarCity → Tesla (2016) | Twitter/X/xAI → SpaceX (2025–26) |
|---|---|---|
| Distressed/loss-making unit absorbed | SolarCity ($768.8M loss, $2.75B debt) | xAI (~$6.35B segment loss); X w/ $13B LBO debt |
| Absorbed by larger Musk vehicle | Tesla | SpaceX |
| Common control / no open-market price | all-stock related-party merger | common-control combinations |
| Debt recast onto acquirer | $2.75B → Tesla | ~$13B → SpaceX |
| Insiders on both sides / recusals | Musk, Rives, Gracias | Musk, Gracias (Valor) |
| History restated into acquirer | SolarCity into Tesla Energy | xAI/X blended; no standalone financials |

The 2016 deal proves the pattern is **repeatable and pre-dates the SpaceX listing by a decade**. Same author, same move, larger stage.

## 5. Falsifiers (what would break H11)

- If SolarCity had been *solvent and growing* when acquired → absorption-of-distress weakened. **Not the case** ($789.9M burn, ~6mo cash).
- If the deal had been arm's-length (independent acquirer, cash price, no insider overlap) → H11 weakened. **Not the case** (all-stock, Musk both sides, cousins running target, insiders holding the target's bonds, recusals).
- If SolarCity's debt/history had been resolved *separately* rather than rolled into Tesla → H11 weakened. **Not the case** (folded into Tesla Energy).

**One line:** SolarCity is the clean, fully-disclosed prototype — a distressed, loss-making, $2.75B-in-debt company run by Musk's cousins, kept alive by insider/SpaceX bond purchases, then absorbed all-stock into the public company Musk controls (with Musk recusing) — exactly the absorb-and-restate move later run at 10× scale to build the SpaceX that listed in 2026.
