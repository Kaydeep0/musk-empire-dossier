# H14 — He sets *and* broadcasts the price he sells into

**Thesis under test:** Beyond owning the *narrative*, Musk influences **price formation itself** —
(a) **setting** the mark (private tender prices; founder buying), (b) **controlling the disclosure
channel** the price reacts to (his personal account designated as the official wire), and (c)
**tiering** who pays what (geographic retail pricing). The reflexive loop closes when the same person
is the source, the broadcaster, and the seller.

**Status: SUPPORTED (`b`/`c`).** Strongest of the reflexivity claims — proven directly by an SEC
enforcement record at Tesla and by the SPCX mark-setting mechanics. Provenance: `b` primary filing ·
`c` regulator/court record · `a` our reading.

---

## 1. He controls the disclosure channel (the cleanest proof)

From the SEC's own record (Press Release 2018-226, Sept 29 2018; SEC v. Musk / SEC v. Tesla
complaints): `c`/`b`

- **2013:** Tesla *formally notified the market* that it "intended to use Musk's Twitter account as a
  means of announcing material information about Tesla and encouraging investors to review Musk's
  tweets." → He converted his **personal megaphone into the company's Reg FD disclosure channel.**
- **Aug 7, 2018:** Musk tweeted he could take Tesla private at **$420/share** with "**funding
  secured**." Per the SEC, the deal was uncertain, no specific terms had been discussed with any
  financing partner, and the statement "lacked an adequate basis in fact."
- **Effect:** the tweet "caused Tesla's stock price to jump by over six percent" and "led to
  significant market disruption." → **The channel he owns moved the price directly.**
- **Outcome:** SEC charged **securities fraud**; settled for **$40M total** ($20M Musk + $20M Tesla),
  **Musk removed as Chairman** (ineligible 3 years), **two new independent directors**, and a
  **board committee to oversee Musk's communications.** The remedy itself is a regulator confirming
  the channel was a price-moving instrument that needed controls.

This is H14's core — *price formation reacting to a channel one person owns* — adjudicated by the SEC.

## 2. He broadcasts, then sells (channel → price → monetization)

- **Nov 6, 2021:** Twitter poll asking whether he should sell 10% of his Tesla stake (framed around
  taxes) → followed by **~$16B sold near the all-time high**, while the underlying **10b5-1 plan had
  been adopted Sept 14, 2021 — weeks *before* the poll** (graded in `PHASE3_FINDINGS.md`). `b`
- This is stages 1→6 of the attention→capital model (`MECHANISM_attention_to_capital.md`) firing in
  order: **broadcast → price strength → insider sale**, with the sale mechanism pre-positioned before
  the broadcast. The poll didn't *cause* a decision; it *narrated* one already scheduled.

## 3. He sets the private mark (SPCX `b`)

From the SPCX dossier / 424(b)(4):
- Musk **personally bought ~$1.42B of employee stock in 2025**, helping set the private mark that
  feeds the **$135 IPO** reference. The founder's own bid *is* the comp for the last private round.
- Material SpaceX news flows through the **owned @SpaceX / @elonmusk channel**, not the wires.
- **Tiered retail pricing:** US $135 vs EU retail max ~$162 — the same share priced higher to the
  retail tier that gets the least vote.

## 4. Verdict, scope, and falsifier

- **"Broadcasts/moves the price"** is proven at **Tesla** by an SEC fraud finding (§1) — the highest
  evidentiary grade available short of an admission (the settlement was no-admit/no-deny). `c`
- **"Sets the price"** is strongest at **SpaceX** (private marks, founder buying, tiered pricing). `b`
- **Combined**, the same person is source + broadcaster + seller across both vehicles.
- **Counter (stated first):** founders buy their own stock (conviction); CEOs use social media;
  global tranches are normal. True — the claim is only that *concentrating* mark-setting + channel +
  audience + the sell decision in **one person** is unusual and self-reinforcing.
- **Falsifier:** if price/information formation were genuinely independent of him — material news via
  neutral wires, marks set by arm's-length rounds, no sales following his broadcasts — H14 would fail.
  The 2013 channel designation, the 2018 fraud finding, and the 2021 poll→sale sequence are the
  opposite of that.

**One line:** a U.S. securities regulator has already found, on the record, that Musk moved Tesla's
price through a channel he owns and Tesla had designated as official — H14 isn't a theory here, it's
an enforced fact, and SPCX shows the same person now also *sets* the mark he broadcasts.
