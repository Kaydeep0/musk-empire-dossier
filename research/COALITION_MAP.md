## 3. One-page outreach / advocacy plan

**Goal.** Get the *sourced, falsifiable* structural findings in front of the people who set proxy
votes, disclosure rules, and informed coverage — so the public buying these securities can price the
structure with eyes open. **Educate and document; never target a person or coordinate markets.**

**Track A — Policy/regulatory (weeks 1–6, LEAD)**
1. **Productize the evidence.** Turn the dossier into a 2-page "structure brief" (claim → primary-source
   citation → falsifier) + the charts. Lead with what is *documented*, label what is *inference* (`a`).
2. **File an SEC comment letter** on the relevant open rulemaking (dual-class disclosure, controlled-company
   exemptions, IPO lock-up/float transparency). Public record, name + firm, with the required disclosures.
3. **Brief governance standard-setters** (CII) and 2–3 asset owners (NBIM-style) with the brief — *information,
   not instruction*. Offer the falsifiers so they can test it independently.
4. **Engage academics** (Bebchuk/Damodaran-tier) to review for rigor; a critique that survives expert review
   is far stronger downstream.

**Track B — Coalition/media (weeks 6–12, FOLLOW)**
5. **Single trusted reporter first.** Offer the *primary-source dossier* (not conclusions) to one
   investigative desk already on the beat. Let them verify independently.
6. **Publish the methodology** (your newsletter) with every figure linked to EDGAR and the strongest
   counter-argument stated first — the same discipline as the SPCX piece.
7. **Convene a loose coalition** of governance orgs (CII, As You Sow, Better Markets) around *disclosure
   reform*, not around any individual. Shared ask: legibility (standalone financials, lock-up transparency,
   related-party clarity).

**Success metrics:** comment letter on the record; ≥2 institutions engage; ≥1 expert review; ≥1
independent story; a concrete disclosure ask adopted by a governance body.

## 4. The shared, defensible "ask" (keep everything pointed here)
Not "Musk is bad" — but **"make the structure legible to the public that buys it"**:
- Standalone financials for absorbed units (no blended-only histories).
- Plain-language lock-up/float-release calendars (the dated unlock test).
- Clear related-party registries with pricing basis (H13).
- Use-of-proceeds line items where IPO cash repays prior-deal debt (H7/H12).
- Sunset provisions on super-voting classes (P2 control wedge).

This ask is pro-disclosure, viewpoint-neutral, and survives the "you're just short the stock" rebuttal.

## 5. Compliance guardrails (read before any outreach)
- **No defamation.** State only what primary filings show; label inference as inference. Avoid claims of
  intent, fraud, or illegality — the filings can't show intent, and the thesis explicitly doesn't allege it.
- **No market coordination.** Do **not** coordinate trades, positions, or timing with short-sellers or any
  group. Acting together re: shares can trigger **13D "group"** status and anti-fraud/manipulation exposure.
  Treat market critics as public-record commentary only.
- **No private data.** Public/professional channels only (org sites, press desks, SEC portals, faculty
  pages, public handles). No personal addresses, no scraping, no contacting family/employees.
- **Your own disclosures.** Anything published under Watts Advisor / a registered capacity needs the
  firm's WSP review + the required IAR/firm/FINRA-SIPC disclosure line, and the "education, not advice;
  not an offer" language. Route public-facing pieces through Gary before posting.
- **Stay falsifiable.** Lead with the counter-argument and the falsifier every time (the method *is* the
  credibility). If a finding fails its falsifier, retract it.
