The hypothesis we are testing

Across a chain of companies, Musk may run a repeatable loop: build real-economy hype, own the channel that broadcasts it, list or absorb into a vehicle he controls, let belief inflate the stock, then sell his stake into the demand that loop created and roll proceeds into the next venture. We are not claiming fraud or intent. We are testing whether the same structural moves recur and whether dated public events confirm or falsify each piece.

Frontier (Tesla, SpaceX, Twitter/X) → Megaphone (X, Grok, owned disclosure channels) → Public vehicle (IPO or absorption) → Control wedge (vote ≫ economics) → Belief premium (high multiple funds the gap) → Exit (Form 4 sales, lock-up releases) → Roll (debt + cash into next vehicle)

Why the SpaceX IPO (SPCX) matters now

SpaceX listed as SPCX on June 16, 2026 at $135/share. It is the latest turn of the loop: xAI and X were folded in, ~$13B of Twitter LBO debt traveled with them, a $20B Goldman bridge was added, and IPO proceeds repaid ~$19B of that stack.
